Basics of First Aid

DRABC is an acronym to describe the procedures used by first-aiders when providing first aid:

  • D

    • Danger
    Always check the danger to you, any bystanders and then the injured or ill person. Make sure you do not put yourself in danger when going to the assistance of another person.

  • R

    • Response from the casualty:
    1. Try to get a response from the casualty- do not shake them get a history.
    2. Identify how the casualty feel.
    3. Check the physical condition of the casualty.
    4. Be alert to the possibility of shock.
    5. Assess vital signs & physical condition of casualty.

  • A

    • Airway
    1. Check airway is free of obstructions.
    2. If casualty is not breathing, open airway.
    3. Remove visible obstructions from mouth & nose.
    4. Obstructions prevent breathing and impede provision of rescue breathing.

  • B

    • Breathing
    1. Check breathing: Look, listen, and feel for breathing for up to 10 seconds.
    2. If not breathing: Administer rescue breathing/mouth-to-mouth resuscitation /EAR. If breathing, monitor.
    3. Assess vital signs: Watch chest/stomach rise & fall for 15 seconds. Count rises, multiply by four.
    4. Record: Note your findings.

  • C

    • Circulation
    1. No pulse: Absence indicates no heartbeat and no circulation.
    2. No pulse detected: Start chest compressions immediately.
    3. No pulse and not breathing: Administer CPR without delay immediately.

CPR

CPR circulates blood and delivers oxygen until specialized medical help arrives. It sustains vital organs, including the brain, in critical moments.

Your Guide to Philippine Emergency Numbers
In case of emergency, call this emergency hotlines: 
        - National Emergency Hotline - 911        
        - Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP)- (02) 8426-0246 / (02) 8426-0219
        - National Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Council (NDRRMC) - 911-1406 / 912-2665 / 912-5668
        - Philippine National Police (PNP) - 117 / (2) 8722-0650
        - Philippine Red Cross - 143
