Walls of Past




On February 15, 2022, we visited the oldest district and historical center in the city of Manila, Intramuros. Before reaching Intramuros, we had to traverse the road amidst the intense glare of the sun. We walked along the length of Padre Burgos Avenue. Upon reaching Intramuros, the sense of wonder overshadowed the fatigue we felt from the long journey. The walls bore the marks of years and decades gone by. It was remarkable because despite its age, it stood resilient. Infrastructure with Spanish influences welcomed us, making us feel like we were transported to the past.

Many tourists visited that day. Despite the intense heat, their joy in capturing photos was evident. Everyone was delighted by the sight of old houses, as if we were transported back to ancient times. Despite the subtle touch of modernization, it cannot be denied that some buildings, especially the famous sites within Intramuros, have been well-preserved. There were also various food vendors scattered along the streets within Intramuros. Horse-drawn carriages ran through the streets in the heat of noon, carrying tourists to their desired destinations. We also took some photos of the streets before continuing our exploration.

Inside Intramuros, there is a grand church, the Manila Cathedral. The interior is spacious, and the designs of the church are beautiful. It symbolizes our Christian identity influenced by the Spaniards.

After entering the church, we visited Fort Santiago, the former headquarters of the colonial military. It is vast and filled with many trees and plants, creating a fresh and cool atmosphere. Despite the beautiful appearance of this place, it holds a hidden and complicated history. During our exploration, we saw many ruined buildings, some reduced to piles of stones. It is saddening to think about what happened during that time.

At the end of Fort Santiago, there is a dungeon or prison near the Pasig River. It is a secret passage underground. We entered with a tour guide. Upon entering, we were greeted by a small space with a low ceiling and an area almost untouched by sunlight. Without the erected lights inside and the dug-up windows, it would surely be enveloped in darkness. There were statues of Filipinos and Americans captured in the corners of each wall. According to our tour guide, they died from hunger and difficulty in breathing due to the war. Cruelty prevailed in this prison. I couldn't help but imagine how the people imprisoned in this small and dark space would scream and cry for help.

It is sad that Intramuros holds a hidden melancholic history. But it is gratifying to have stepped on those places rich in lessons and Philippine history. As a student, it is crucial to be aware of our country's past and our honorable heroes. It may not be a place with picturesque views or air-conditioned like malls and attractions in Manila, but it is one of the places where new knowledge about our nation can be gained. Intramuros is a historic place, not just because it is the oldest city in Manila but because of the experiences and lessons it imparts to the youth. The walls of Intramuros symbolize the resilience of Filipinos from then until now. But within its sturdy facade, it holds a bitter truth, the harsh fate of Filipinos in the past.


Women




In the past years, women have experienced severe discrimination. Some perceive women as weak compared to men, leading to unequal opportunities in education, employment, and within the household. Their human rights are violated due to the discrimination and inequality they continue to face in their own country. Nevertheless, women have proven that they are not weak because they have fought for their rights and demonstrated their capabilities.

Currently, women have proven their capabilities in various fields. Through the courage shown by women, they slowly gain recognition from society, proving that they are more than just women. Our female heroes have long proven that we can do what men do. They showcased their unique courage and greatness in resisting invaders. Women are considered the light of the home, not only because they take care of household chores but also because they are the ones who first nurture the minds of their families. They are the light that will guide the future of children through their advice, guidance, and love. Being a parent is not easy, but the exemplary mothers' ability is admirable. They also proved their capabilities in various fields. Some women today take on jobs that are traditionally seen as male-dominated, such as in construction. Here, women have proven that they can do activities typically associated with men. They not only demonstrated that they can keep up physically but also showcased their intellectual prowess. Many women now pursue careers in medicine, education, business, and even in politics. Numerous women have been appointed as leaders in our country, where they display their talent, dedication, love, and ability to lead the nation. These are just a few of the achievements of women in our country, and they can accomplish much more if we continue to support them.

Women continue to demonstrate their excellence in various fields. They bear significant responsibilities and duties in our society, but they have proven to be strong and resilient. Capability knows no gender because what men can do, women can also do. May we give equal consideration to different genders. Let us stand up for our rights because women are capable of excelling, not just as women but as individuals.
